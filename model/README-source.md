# Source & method note — Cameroon surfaces

**What this documents:** how the material in `surfaces/ai-truth-kamerun.html` (PL) and
`ai-truth-kamerun-en.html` (EN) was produced, so a reader can judge its weight rather than
take it on trust.

## Method
White-source OSINT only. Every substantive statement on the surfaces carries an inline link to
a publicly reachable source — there are **53 source links** across the pages. Categories used:

- national/regional institutions and instruments (e.g. the national CIRT, the Malabo Convention),
- market and connectivity reporting (e.g. DataReportal country reports, telecom trade press),
- legal commentary on data-protection compliance,
- operator and ecosystem announcements.

No private data, no personal data, no non-public material was used. Nothing was inferred into a
figure: where a number could not be sourced, it is absent rather than estimated.

## Evidence discipline (`claim ≤ proof`)
- A cited indicator is a **claim with a source**, not our measurement. We did not re-run any
  survey or index; we report what the cited body published.
- A gap identified in the analysis is an **observation about the public record**, not a statement
  that no such capability exists privately.
- Identifying an opportunity is **not** a claim that any engagement exists or is planned.

## Freshness
This is a **snapshot** taken at the time of research. Indicators (connectivity, rankings, legal
status) move; none of it refreshes itself. Re-check each cited source before relying on any value.

## Scope boundary
Non-EU track, kept deliberately separate from the EU material so the EU sovereignty scope is not
diluted. Nothing here is a deployed service — the surfaces are static documents.
